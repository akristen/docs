Docker subscriptions provide licensing and usage entitlements for Docker
products. After [creating your account](/manuals/accounts/create-account.md),
you can purchase several subscription types that extend your Docker product
usage.

## Docker subscription types

Docker subscriptions fall into two broad categories: plans and product licenses.

Plans are tiered, per-user subscriptions that cover the core Docker development
platform. Each plan tier includes a defined feature set and usage allowances.
Tiers range from a free option for individual developers to paid tiers for
teams and enterprises.

Product licenses are purchased separately from plans and add specific
capabilities to your account. Available products include an AI development
assistant, hardened base images, and ephemeral cloud development environments.
Some products are organized into their own tiers with distinct feature sets.
Others are prepaid bundles or pay-as-you-go offerings. You can add product
licenses to any plan tier.

To compare what each plan tier and product includes, see the
[product catalog](/manuals/subscription/products/). To make a purchase, see
[Purchase subscriptions](/manuals/subscription/purchase-subscriptions/).

## Scale subscription

Most subscriptions include a fixed amount of usage. You can purchase additional
units to extend usage without changing your plan tier.

Purchasable units include:

- Seats: additional users added to your organization. Each seat extends the
  subscription entitlements to one more member.
- Product licenses: access to specific products or product tiers, purchased
  separately from your core plan. A license may be scoped to a repository, a
  prepaid credit bundle, or a monthly entitlement.
- Build minutes: additional cloud build capacity, sold in blocks and consumed
  within the billing period.
- Runtime minutes: additional cloud test runtime, available as a subscription
  block or on demand.
- Repositories: additional container repositories covered by security scanning
  and analysis features.

## Support add-ons

Standard support is included with paid plans and scales with plan tier. Docker
also offers premium support as an optional add-on for Docker Business and Docker
Hardened Images customers. Premium support provides 24/7 response, priority
SLAs, and access to a dedicated technical advisory manager.

For details, see
[Docker Premium Support and TAM Service](https://www.docker.com/pricing/premium-support-tam/).
